Sustainable Development Goals

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a universal set of 17 global goals set by the United Nations in 2015, designed to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure prosperity for all by 2030. Each goal has specific targets to achieve, addressing the most pressing issues facing our world, such as climate change, inequality, and environmental degradation.
